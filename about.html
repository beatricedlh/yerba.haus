Yerba mate is a traditional South American drink made by steeping dried leaves in a gourd and sharing it with friends and family.

This website follows the ritual of drinking mate step by step, with each stage opening outward into larger cultural, ecological, political, and social systems.

how to make the perfect cup of mate

step 1

Fill up your gourd ½ or ¾ of the way with yerba mate depending on your preference.

step 2

Cover the mouth of the gourd with your hand, turn it over and shake it to even out the consistency of the yerba mate.

step 3

The loose leaf yerba mate inside should remain at a 45° angle so you have space for the bombilla and water.

step 4

Gently pour hot water into the gourd close to the bombilla.

step 5

Sip until you finish the gourd, fill it up with water again, and pass to a friend.

What is yerba mate?

Yerba mate is a traditional South American beverage made from the dried leaves of the Ilex paraguariensis plant. It is commonly consumed in countries such as Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, and Brazil, and is known for its naturally occurring caffeine and antioxidants.

What does yerba mate taste like?

Yerba mate has a naturally bitter, earthy flavour that can vary depending on the blend, preparation method, and region of origin.

Does yerba mate contain caffeine?

Yes. Yerba mate naturally contains caffeine, as well as other compounds that contribute to its stimulating effects.

Is yerba mate consumed daily?

In many regions, yerba mate is part of daily routines and social rituals rather than an occasional beverage.
